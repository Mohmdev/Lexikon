# Warum Endungen — why adjectives take endings

## The situation

After *sein* ("to be"), the adjective is **bare**:

> Das Auto ist **schnell**. — The car is fast.

In front of the noun, the adjective takes an ending, and the ending varies:

> das **schnelle** Auto — the fast car
> ein **schnelles** Auto — a fast car

Same word *schnell*, same noun, two different endings (-e, -es). This is governed by **one rule**.

---

## The one rule: case and gender are marked once per phrase

A German noun phrase must show **its case and gender**. This marking can sit on the **article** or on
the **adjective**, but the phrase only needs it **once**. Which element carries it depends on whether
the article already provides it.

**Article marks it clearly → adjective takes a minimal ending.**
*der* is unambiguously masculine, *das* unambiguously neuter. The case/gender is already shown, so the
adjective takes a near-empty ending, **-e** or **-en**:
> der **schnelle** Wagen — the fast car
These are the **weak** endings (file 02).

**No article → adjective carries the marking.**
With nothing in front, the adjective takes a **strong** ending matching what the article would have
shown:
> **kaltes** Wasser — cold water
The **-es** is the *das* marking ("das Wasser") moved onto the adjective. These are the **strong**
endings (file 04).

**Article *ein* marks it partially → adjective fills the gaps.**
*ein* alone cannot distinguish masculine from neuter (*ein* fits both *der Wagen* and *das Auto*), so
the adjective supplies what *ein* leaves unmarked:
> ein **schneller** Wagen — a fast car *(adjective shows masculine)*
> ein **schnelles** Auto — a fast car *(adjective shows neuter)*
These are the **mixed** endings (file 03).

---

## Summary

Three tables, **one principle**: case and gender are marked once per phrase, by whichever element is
free to carry it. To decide an ending, check:

> *Does the article already show case and gender?*
> **Yes** → minimal adjective ending: -e / -en.
> **No** → adjective takes the strong, article-like ending.
