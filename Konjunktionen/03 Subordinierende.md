# Subordinierende Konjunktionen — the verb goes to the end

These open a **dependent clause** — one that can't stand on its own — and send the conjugated verb all
the way to the **back** of that clause. One rule covers the entire list:

> **subordinating conjunction → … → conjugated verb (last word)**

> Ich bleibe zu Hause, **weil** es heute stark **regnet**. — I'm staying home because it's raining hard.
> Sie fragt, **ob** der Zug pünktlich **kommt**. — She asks whether the train comes on time.

Comma **before** the conjunction, always. If you front the clause, the main verb hops to right after the
comma (the *verb, comma, verb* collision from [[01 Wortstellung]]).

The list is long, so don't memorize it flat — **sort it by what it means.** Then it's six small,
sensible groups instead of one blur.

---

## 1. Cause / reason — "because, since"

| German | English | example |
|---|---|---|
| **weil** | because | Ich bleibe, **weil** ich müde **bin**. |
| **da** | since (reason already known; more formal) | **Da** es **regnet**, bleiben wir. |

*(For the "because" that does **not** flip the verb, that's coordinating **denn** → [[02 Koordinierende]].)*

---

## 2. Time — "when, while, before, after, until…"

The biggest group. The trio **wenn / als** (and the question word **wann**) is such a common mix-up it
gets its own file → [[04 wenn als wann ob]].

| German | English | example |
|---|---|---|
| **wenn** | when / whenever (repeated or future) | **Wenn** es **regnet**, bleibe ich. |
| **als** | when (a single past event) | **Als** ich klein **war**, … |
| **während** | while / whereas | **Während** sie **kocht**, lese ich. |
| **bevor** | before | **Bevor** ich **gehe**, esse ich. |
| **nachdem** | after *(triggers the tense-step → [[12 Plusquamperfekt]])* | **Nachdem** wir **gegessen hatten**, gingen wir. |
| **seit / seitdem** | since (a point in time) | **Seit** ich hier **wohne**, bin ich glücklich. |
| **bis** | until | Warte, **bis** ich **komme**. |
| **sobald** | as soon as | **Sobald** er **kommt**, essen wir. |
| **solange** | as long as | **Solange** es **regnet**, bleibe ich. |

---

## 3. Condition — "if, in case"

| German | English | example |
|---|---|---|
| **wenn** | if (a condition) | **Wenn** du **willst**, helfe ich. |
| **falls** | in case / if (less certain) | **Falls** es **regnet**, nimm den Schirm. |
| **sofern** | provided that (formal) | Ich helfe, **sofern** ich **kann**. |

*(Note **wenn** does double duty — time **and** condition. Context decides.)*

---

## 4. Concession — "although, even though"

| German | English | example |
|---|---|---|
| **obwohl** | although | Ich gehe, **obwohl** ich müde **bin**. |
| **obgleich / obschon** | although (formal) | Ich bleibe, **obgleich** es spät **ist**. |
| **wenngleich** | even if / although (literary) | Es half, **wenngleich** es teuer **war**. |

---

## 5. Purpose & result — "so that"

The classic confusable pair — **damit** (intended goal) vs **sodass** (actual outcome):

| German | English | example |
|---|---|---|
| **damit** | so that *(purpose — the aim)* | Ich lerne, **damit** ich die Prüfung **bestehe**. |
| **sodass** | so that *(result — what followed)* | Es regnete, **sodass** wir zu Hause **blieben**. |

Test: **damit** answers *"with what goal?"*; **sodass** answers *"and what was the consequence?"*
*(When subject is the same, purpose often goes to an **um … zu** infinitive instead → [[06 Infinitive Clauses]].)*

---

## 6. Manner & comparison — "that, whether, by, as if"

The workhorses that embed a statement or describe *how*:

| German | English | example |
|---|---|---|
| **dass** | that | Ich weiß, **dass** er **kommt**. |
| **ob** | whether (hidden yes/no question) | Ich frage, **ob** er **kommt**. |
| **indem** | by (doing) — means/method | Er lernt, **indem** er viel **liest**. |
| **als ob** | as if | Er tut, **als ob** er **schläft**. |
| **ohne dass** | without (…happening) | Er ging, **ohne dass** ich es **merkte**. |
| **anstatt dass** | instead of (…doing) | **Anstatt dass** er **hilft**, schläft er. |

*(**dass** is the most common subordinator in the language — the all-purpose "that" that embeds one
clause inside another.)*

---

## The strategy

One rule for all of them — **verb to the end**. The only real learning is **vocabulary by group**:
grab the everyday members first (**weil, dass, wenn, als, ob, obwohl, damit, bevor, nachdem**), and let
the formal cousins (*da, sofern, obgleich, wenngleich*) come later. The grammar never changes.
→ [[01 Wortstellung]]
