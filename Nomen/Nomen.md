# Nomen (nouns)

A noun names a **thing** — a person, object, place, or idea: *Mann, Tisch, Stadt, Freiheit*. Every
German noun carries a **gender** and a **plural** that are not fully predictable. Both must be learned
with the word.

---

## Two fixed facts

- **Every noun is capitalized** — *der **Hund**, die **Freiheit**, das **Auto***. This applies to all
  nouns, not just names. When reading, a capital letter mid-sentence usually marks the start of a noun.
- **In the plural, the article is always *die*** — gender does not mark in the plural
  (*der Tisch / die Lampe / das Haus* → all **die**).

---

## Learn the triple

A noun's gender and plural are mostly raw facts, not derivable from rules. Encode them **at first
contact with the word**, as a three-part unit:

> **article + singular + plural** → *das Haus, die Häuser*

Recording gender and plural at first contact is cheap; retrofitting them onto thousands of nouns later
is a common cause of stalled progress at B1. The word list is built around this triple →
[[Nomen 001-100]].

---

## Topics, foundational first

### 1. Gender — der / die / das

Gender drives articles, adjective endings, and pronouns. Mostly a raw fact, but a set of **reliable
ending-rules** (-ung → die, -chen → das…) predicts much of it.
→ [[01 Genus]]

### 2. Plural — the five patterns

German forms plurals five ways (-e, -en, -er, -s, -∅), semi-predictable from gender and ending. The
second part of the triple.
→ [[02 Plural]]

### 3. Compounds

*Hand + Schuh → Handschuh.* The **last** noun sets the gender and plural for the whole word, so a long
compound takes its grammar from its final element.
→ [[03 Komposita]]

### 4. Cases on the noun

Case mostly marks on the article, not the noun. The noun itself takes an ending in exactly **three**
places: Genitiv -s, Dativ plural -n, and the n-declension.
→ [[04 Deklination]]

### 5. Wortbildung — building nouns

Most nouns are **built** from a verb or adjective plus a standard suffix, and each suffix also fixes
the gender. *wohnen → die Wohnung, schön → die Schönheit, der Hund → das Hündchen.* This multiplies
vocabulary and supplies the gender at the same time.
→ [[05 Wortbildung]]
