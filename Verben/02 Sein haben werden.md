# sein, haben, werden

These three need separate treatment: they are the **most common verbs in the language**, they are
**irregular** (the present-tense rules from [[01 Präsens]] do not fully apply), and each also serves
as an **auxiliary** (helper verb) that builds other tenses.

---

## sein — to be

The most irregular verb. Memorize the forms.

| person | form | English |
| ------ | ---- | ------- |
| ich | **bin** | I am |
| du | **bist** | you are |
| er / sie / es | **ist** | he/she/it is |
| wir | **sind** | we are |
| ihr | **seid** | you are |
| sie / Sie | **sind** | they / you are |

> Ich **bin** müde. — I am tired.
> Wir **sind** zu Hause. — We are at home.

## haben — to have

Mostly regular, but the **du** and **er** forms drop the **b** (*hab* → *ha*):

| person | form | English |
| ------ | ---- | ------- |
| ich | **habe** | I have |
| du | **hast** | you have |
| er / sie / es | **hat** | he/she/it has |
| wir | **haben** | we have |
| ihr | **habt** | you have |
| sie / Sie | **haben** | they / you have |

> Ich **habe** Zeit. — I have time.
> Sie **hat** einen Hund. — She has a dog.

## werden — to become

The odd forms are **du wirst** and **er wird** (the stem flips to *wir-*):

| person | form | English |
| ------ | ---- | ------- |
| ich | **werde** | I become |
| du | **wirst** | you become |
| er / sie / es | **wird** | he/she/it becomes |
| wir | **werden** | we become |
| ihr | **werdet** | you become |
| sie / Sie | **werden** | they / you become |

> Es **wird** kalt. — It's getting cold. *(werden = to get / become)*
> Ich **werde** Arzt. — I'm becoming a doctor.

---

## Auxiliary uses

Each verb has its plain meaning and a second role building another tense:

- **haben** → builds the **Perfekt** (the everyday past) for most verbs: *ich **habe** gegessen* (I
  ate). → [[05 Perfekt]]
- **sein** → builds the **Perfekt** for verbs of **motion / change of state**: *ich **bin** gegangen*
  (I went). → [[05 Perfekt]]
- **werden** → builds the **Futur** (*ich **werde** kommen* — I will come, → [[07 Futur]]) and the
  **passive** (*das Haus **wird** gebaut* — the house is being built).

---

## Präteritum — the simple past used in speech

Spoken German normally avoids the simple past (Präteritum) and uses Perfekt instead. **These three
are the exception**: even in conversation, *war / hatte / wurde* are used rather than the perfect.

| verb | Präteritum (ich-form) | English |
| ---- | --------------------- | ------- |
| sein | **war** | was |
| haben | **hatte** | had |
| werden | **wurde** | became |

> Ich **war** müde. — I was tired.
> Ich **hatte** keine Zeit. — I had no time.

Full conjugation of these in [[06 Präteritum]].
