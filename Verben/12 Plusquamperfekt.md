# Plusquamperfekt — the "had done" past

The Plusquamperfekt is the **Perfekt** ([[05 Perfekt]]) with **one change**: the auxiliary verb moves
into the past.

| tense | helper | example |
|---|---|---|
| **Perfekt** | haben/sein in the **present** | Ich **habe** gegessen. — I have eaten. |
| **Plusquamperfekt** | haben/sein in the **Präteritum** | Ich **hatte** gegessen. — I **had** eaten. |

Form: **hatte / war + Partizip II.** Same participle and same bracket (participle at the end,
[[02 Word Order]]); only the auxiliary changes: *habe→hatte*, *bin→war* (see [[06 Präteritum]]).

---

## Function: a past before the past

Plusquamperfekt marks an action that was **already finished before another past moment**. English uses
"had" the same way:

> Der Zug **war** schon **abgefahren**, als ich ankam.
> — The train **had** already **left** when I arrived. *(leaving happened before arriving)*

With two past events, this tense marks **which one came first**.

---

## Auxiliary: same haben/sein rule as Perfekt

The auxiliary choice is unchanged: verbs of **motion / change of state** take **war** (sein), the rest
take **hatte** (haben). Same split as [[05 Perfekt]]:

> Ich **hatte** das Buch **gelesen**. — I had read the book. *(haben verb → hatte)*
> Er **war** nach Hause **gegangen**. — He had gone home. *(motion → war)*

---

## Primary use: nachdem

With **nachdem** ("after"), Plusquamperfekt is almost obligatory. German uses a **tense step**: the
earlier action goes in Plusquamperfekt, the later one in Präteritum (or Perfekt):

> **Nachdem** ich **gegessen hatte**, *(Plusquamperfekt — first)*
> **ging** ich spazieren. *(Präteritum — second)*
> — After I had eaten, I went for a walk.

Pattern to memorize: **nachdem + Plusquamperfekt → main clause in Präteritum.** (nachdem is a
subordinating conjunction, so its verb goes to the end — [[Konjunktionen]].)

---

## Register: when to use it

In casual speech, Germans often chain two Perfekts and let *nachdem/schon/vorher* signal the order.
Plusquamperfekt is mainly used in **writing and narrative**, where marking "this happened first"
matters. Use it especially with *nachdem*.
