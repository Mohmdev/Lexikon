# TeKaMoLo — adverb / adverbial word order

**The rule:** when several adverbials pile up in one clause, German orders them
**Te → Ka → Mo → Lo**:

| Order | Type | Symbol | Question | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Te**mporal | ⏰ | wann? (when) | heute, oft, um 8 Uhr |
| 2 | **Ka**usal | 🔗 | warum? (why) | deshalb, wegen des Regens |
| 3 | **Mo**dal | ⚙️ | wie? (how) | gern, schnell, mit dem Bus |
| 4 | **Lo**kal | 📍 | wo/wohin? (where) | hier, nach Hause, ins Kino |

## Example

> Ich fahre **heute** (⏰) **wegen der Arbeit** (🔗) **mit dem Bus** (⚙️) **in die Stadt** (📍).
> = I'm going to the city today, because of work, by bus.

Drop any of them and the rest keep their relative order:

> Ich fahre **heute** (⏰) **mit dem Bus** (⚙️) **nach Hause** (📍).

## Why this earns its keep

The type tag in each adverb entry (⏰🔗⚙️📍) **is** its position in this sequence. So tagging a
word by type isn't just filing — it tells you where the word lands in a sentence. One rule learned
here = correct ordering for every adverb you'll ever tag. (Types are defined in [[01-type-taxonomy]].)

## Edges / notes

- **Te–Ka–Mo–Lo is the default, not a law.** Front the element you want to stress and it jumps to
  position 1 (before the verb) for emphasis: *Heute fahre ich …* / *Mit dem Bus fahre ich …*
  (Verb stays in slot 2 — that's the V2 rule, see top-level `Grammatik/` #02.)
- 🔆 **Grad** (degree: sehr, ganz) is **not** in this sequence — it clings to the adjective/adverb it
  intensifies, it's not a standalone adverbial slot.
- This governs **adverbials** generally (single-word adverbs *and* prepositional phrases like "mit dem
  Bus"), not just one-word adverbs.
