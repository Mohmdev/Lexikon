# Konjunktionaladverbien — connectors that trigger inversion

These adverbs **link two clauses**, like English "therefore, however, then, otherwise." They connect
the new sentence to the preceding idea:

> Es regnet. **Deshalb** bleibe ich zu Hause. — It's raining. **Therefore** I'm staying home.

They are treated separately not for their *meaning* but for their effect on **word order**.

## Rule: they fill the Vorfeld, so the verb inverts

A connector adverb fills slot 1 (the Vorfeld). By the rule that the **verb stays in slot 2**
([[03 Vorfeld und Inversion]]), the subject moves behind the verb:

| 1 — Vorfeld | 2 — VERB | 3 |
|---|---|---|
| Ich | bleibe | zu Hause. |
| **Deshalb** | **bleibe** | **ich** zu Hause. |

> ✅ **Deshalb bleibe ich** zu Hause. — NOT ❌ "Deshalb ich bleibe."

English keeps the subject first ("Therefore, **I stay**…"), which produces the wrong order here. The
German pattern is **connector → verb → subject.**

---

## Three connector types compared

German has **three** kinds of joining words. They look similar in English but have different effects
on the verb:

| type | examples | effect on word order |
|---|---|---|
| **coordinating conjunction** | und, aber, oder, denn | sits *outside* — **no slot, no change** |
| **konjunktional adverb** | deshalb, trotzdem, dann | fills slot 1 — **verb second → inversion** |
| **subordinating conjunction** | weil, dass, wenn, obwohl | **kicks the verb to the END** |

The same idea in all three:

> Ich bin müde, **aber ich bleibe**. — …but I'm staying. *(coordinating → subject then verb)*
> Ich bin müde. **Trotzdem bleibe ich**. — …still, I'm staying. *(adverb → verb then subject)*
> Ich bleibe, **obwohl ich müde bin**. — …although I'm tired. *(subordinating → verb to the end)*

One English "but/although," three German word orders. Connector adverbs are flagged separately
because they look like conjunctions but behave like adverbs: they occupy a sentence position. The
conjunctions are in [[Konjunktionen]]; the full V2/verb-to-end rules are in [[02 Word Order]].

## Common connectors by meaning

- **cause / result:** deshalb, deswegen, darum, daher, also, folglich → *that's why…*
- **contrast:** trotzdem, dennoch, jedoch, allerdings → *nevertheless / however*
- **addition:** außerdem, zudem, ebenfalls → *besides / also*
- **condition / alternative:** sonst, andernfalls → *otherwise*
- **time / sequence:** dann, danach, zuerst, schließlich, inzwischen → *then / afterwards*

These are filed by meaning in [[Kausaladverbien]] (logical) and [[Temporaladverbien]] (sequence),
each tagged ⛓️ to mark that it triggers inversion.

---

## Recap

- A konjunktional adverb **connects** *and* **takes slot 1** → so the **verb comes next, subject after**.
- Don't confuse with *und/aber* (no flip) or *weil/dass* (verb to the end).
- *Deshalb **bleibe ich***, *trotzdem **gehe ich***, *dann **rufe ich** an* — connector, verb, subject.
