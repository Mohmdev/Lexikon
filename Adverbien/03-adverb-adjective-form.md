# Adverb = adjective (one word, two jobs)

## First — what's the difference between an adjective and an adverb?

A "describing word" can point at two different targets:

- It can describe a **thing** (a noun):
  > a **fast** car  →  "fast" describes the *car* (a thing). **This use is called an ADJECTIVE.**

- It can describe an **action** (a verb):
  > he drives **fast**  →  "fast" describes the *driving* (an action). **This use is called an ADVERB.**

Same idea — "fastness" — just aimed at two different things: a *thing* vs an *action*. That's the
whole adjective/adverb distinction. Adjective → points at a noun. Adverb → points at a verb.

## English is messy about this — sometimes it changes the word

Watch what English does when you switch from describing a thing to describing an action:

| describing a thing (adjective) | describing an action (adverb) | changed? |
|---|---|---|
| a **fast** car | he drives **fast** | no — same word |
| a **quick** car | he drives **quickly** | yes — added **-ly** |
| **good** food | she cooks **well** | yes — whole new word |

So English sometimes makes you learn a *second* form (quick→quickly, good→well).

## German is clean — the word NEVER changes

In German, the plain adjective is **automatically** also the adverb. No -ly, no second word to learn.

| German word | describing a thing (adjective) | describing an action (adverb) |
|---|---|---|
| **schnell** | das **schnelle** Auto (the fast car) | das Auto fährt **schnell** (the car drives fast) |
| **gut** | **gutes** Essen (good food) | sie kocht **gut** (she cooks well) |
| **leise** | die **leise** Musik (the quiet music) | sprich **leise** (speak quietly) |

Look at the right column — **schnell, gut, leise** never change to describe the action. Where English
says "well," German just reuses "gut."

## The one small difference you'll notice

In the *thing* column, the word grew an ending: schnell**e**, gut**es**. In the *action* column it's
**bare**: schnell, gut, leise. That's the only visible difference:

- in front of a noun (adjective) → it takes an ending
- describing a verb (adverb) → it stays bare, no ending

(The endings are a separate topic — adjective endings. Ignore them here. The point of *this* page is
just: the **base word is the same**.)

## Consequence

Learn the adjective "schnell = fast" and you already know the adverb "schnell = quickly" — it's the
same word. So manner adverbs that *are* adjectives aren't collected separately; they're adjectives
doing a second job, and they live in the `Adjektive/` section.

Exception: a few real manner/attitude adverbs are **not** adjectives — you can't put them before a
noun (gern, leider, anders, so). Those have nowhere to hide in `Adjektive/`, so they're collected
in [[Modaladverbien]].
