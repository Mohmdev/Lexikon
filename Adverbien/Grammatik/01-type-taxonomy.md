# Adverb types — the full map

Every adverb gets sorted by **two separate questions**. Answer both — they're independent.

## Overview

Five **types** (what it means) + two **flags** (special behavior):

| | Category | Answers / does | ~ count |
|---|---|---|---|
| ⏰ | Temporal (time) | wann? (when) | ~60–100 |
| 📍 | Lokal (place) | wo? (where) | ~30–50 |
| ⚙️ | Modal (manner) | wie? (how) | open — mostly adjectives |
| 🔗 | Kausal (cause) | warum? (why) | ~20–40 |
| 🔆 | Grad (degree) | wie sehr? (how much) | ~15–30 |
| 🧩 | Pronominal *(flag)* | da-/wo- + preposition | ~40–60 (generated) |
| ⛓️ | Konjunktional *(flag)* | connects clauses, flips word order | ~20–40 |

*Counts are rough estimates.*

---

## Question 1 — what does it tell you? (the TYPE)

Five types, each answers a different question about the action.

### ⏰ Temporal — WHEN? (time)
Tells you *when* the action happens.
> Ich komme **heute**. — I'm coming **today**.

Examples: heute (today), oft (often), immer (always), bald (soon), jetzt (now).

### 📍 Lokal — WHERE? (place)
Tells you *where* the action happens, or in what direction.
> Er wartet **hier**. — He's waiting **here**.

Examples: hier (here), dort (there), oben (above), links (on the left).

### ⚙️ Modal — HOW? (manner)
Tells you *the way* the action is done.
> Sie singt **gut**. — She sings **well**.

Most "how" words — schnell (fast), gut (well), leise (quietly) — are really just **adjectives** reused
as adverbs; German doesn't change the word (see [03-adverb-adjective-form.md](./03-adverb-adjective-form.md)).
Those live in `Adjektive/`. Only a few manner words are **pure adverbs** that are never adjectives:
**gern** (gladly), **leider** (unfortunately), **anders** (differently), **so** (like this).

### 🔗 Kausal — WHY? (cause)
Tells you the *reason*, or links a result back to its cause.
> Es regnet, **deshalb** bleibe ich. — It's raining, **so** I'm staying.

Examples: deshalb (therefore), darum (that's why), trotzdem (nevertheless).

### 🔆 Grad — HOW MUCH? (degree)
The odd one out: it does **not** describe the verb/action like the other four. It **clings to another
word** (an adjective or another adverb) and turns its intensity up or down.
> Es ist **sehr** kalt. — It is **very** cold.

Here "sehr" isn't describing an action — it's pumping up the adjective "kalt."
Examples: sehr (very), ganz (quite), zu (too), kaum (hardly).

⏰🔗⚙️📍 line up as the **TeKaMoLo** word-order sequence (see [02-TeKaMoLo.md](./02-TeKaMoLo.md)).
🔆 Grad sits outside it because it isn't a sentence slot — it's glued to whatever it intensifies.

## Question 2 — does it have a special structural behavior? (the FLAGS)

A word already has a type from Question 1, and it *may also* carry one of these two behaviors on top.

### 🧩 Pronominal — a fused da-/wo- word
Words like **damit** (with it), **worauf** (on what). See [04-pronominal.md](./04-pronominal.md).

### ⛓️ Konjunktional — a clause-connector that flips word order
Words like **deshalb**, **trotzdem**. When one starts a clause, the verb jumps in front of the
subject (Deshalb *bleibe ich*…). See [05-konjunktional.md](./05-konjunktional.md).

---

## One word can be two things at once

That's why there are two questions: a single word can carry an answer to **both**. Take **deshalb** —
it holds two separate facts you need at the same time:

1. **What it means** (Question 1) → cause. It answers *warum?* (why).
2. **What it does to the sentence** (Question 2) → it flips the word order: the verb jumps in front
   of the subject.

Watch both happen in one sentence:

> Es regnet. **Deshalb** *bleibe* **ich** zu Hause.
> — It's raining. **That's why** I'm staying home.

"deshalb" tells you the *reason* (meaning = 🔗 Kausal) **and** forces "bleibe" to come before "ich"
(behavior = ⛓️ Konjunktional). Two different facts about the same word — so it needs a type **and** a
flag, not just one label.
