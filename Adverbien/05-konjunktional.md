# Konjunktional adverbs — connectors that flip the word order

## What they are

Some adverbs do a second job: they **bridge two clauses**, like English "therefore, however, then,
otherwise." They glue the new sentence onto the idea before it.

> Es regnet. **Deshalb** bleibe ich zu Hause. — It's raining. **Therefore** I'm staying home.

The reason they get their own flag (⛓️) isn't the meaning — it's that they **mess with word order**.

## The one rule: the verb jumps in front of the subject

German has a hard rule for main clauses: **the conjugated verb is always the 2nd element.** Whatever
sits in 1st place, the verb comes right after it.

Normally the subject sits 1st:

| 1 | 2 (verb) | 3 |
|---|---|---|
| Ich | bleibe | zu Hause. |

Put a konjunktional adverb in 1st place and the rule still holds — verb stays 2nd, so the **subject
gets bumped to 3rd**:

| 1 | 2 (verb) | 3 |
|---|---|---|
| Deshalb | bleibe | ich zu Hause. |

So: **Deshalb bleibe ich** ✅ — NOT "Deshalb ich bleibe" ❌.

## Why English speakers get this wrong

English keeps the subject first after a connector:
> Therefore, **I stay** home.  (connector → subject → verb)

German flips the verb and subject:
> Deshalb **bleibe ich** …  (connector → verb → subject)

Your English instinct will produce the wrong order. This is the reflex to retrain.

## The deeper reason it's its own category (the conjunction trap)

German has TWO kinds of connectors that mean the same in English but behave **oppositely**:

**True conjunctions** (und, aber, oder, denn) — sit *outside* the sentence, don't count as a slot →
**no flip**:
> Ich bin müde, **aber ich bleibe**.  — I'm tired, but I'm staying.  (aber → subject → verb)

**Konjunktional adverbs** (trotzdem, deshalb, dann) — count as the 1st slot → **flip**:
> Ich bin müde, **trotzdem bleibe ich**.  — I'm tired, but still I'm staying.  (trotzdem → verb → subject)

Same English "but," opposite word order. *That's* why these need their own flag: they look like
connecting words but behave like adverbs — they take a real position in the sentence.

## How they're filed in your Lexikon

Not a separate word list. Each one lives in its **meaning** file with a ⛓️ tag:

- logic (cause / contrast / addition): deshalb, deswegen, daher, trotzdem, dennoch, jedoch, außerdem,
  sonst, also → [[Kausaladverbien]]
- sequence (time): dann, danach, zuerst, schließlich, inzwischen → [[Temporaladverbien]]
