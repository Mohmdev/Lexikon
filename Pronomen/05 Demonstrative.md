# Demonstrativpronomen (demonstratives) — "this / that one"

Demonstratives **point** and single something out: *this* book, *that* one, *the* one over there.
The forms reuse declension grids covered elsewhere in this folder.

---

## dieser — "this"

*dieser* (this) declines with the **der/die/das endings** ([[01 Bestimmte]]) — same pattern, *dies-*
stem:

| | maskulin | feminin | neutrum | Plural |
| ------------- | -------- | ------- | ------- | ------ |
| **Nominativ** | dieser | diese | dieses | diese |
| **Akkusativ** | diesen | diese | dieses | diese |
| **Dativ** | diesem | dieser | diesem | diesen |
| **Genitiv** | dieses | dieser | dieses | dieser |

> **Dieser** Mann ist nett. — This man is nice.
> Ich nehme **diese** Tasche. — I'll take this bag.

The counterpart *jener* ("that") exists but sounds bookish. In everyday German, "that" is **der/die/das**
with stress, or *dieser* + context, or **da** (*das Buch **da*** — that book there).

---

## der / die / das as a pointing pronoun

Spoken German uses **der/die/das**, stressed, to mean **"that one / him / her"** — often in place of
a plain personal pronoun, for emphasis. Standing alone, they take the **same forms as the relative
pronouns** ([[04 Relative]]), including *dem, denen, dessen*:

> **Den** kenne ich! — That one (him), I know!
> **Die** ist nett. — She's nice / That one's nice. *(more emphatic than "sie ist nett")*
> Mit **denen** rede ich nicht. — Those people, I don't talk to.

---

## das as an all-purpose "this/that is"

A special, **invariable** *das* introduces or points at something, regardless of gender or number:

> **Das** ist mein Auto. — This/that is my car.
> **Das** sind meine Bücher. — These/those are my books. *(das stays das, even though "Bücher" is plural)*

Do not confuse this fixed pointer *das* with the neuter article *das*. Here it agrees with nothing;
it means "this/that (thing/situation)."

---

Also in this family: **derselbe** "the same," **solcher** "such a."
