# Pronomen (Pronouns)

<!--
Closed set. Personal pronouns are learned BY CASE (Nominativ / Akkusativ / Dativ), not as a flat
list — the case table IS the data. English on the right. Possessives (mein, dein…) and others below.
-->

## Personalpronomen (Personal pronouns) — by case

| Nominativ (subject) | Akkusativ (direct object) | Dativ (to / for) | English |
|---|---|---|---|
| ich | mich | mir | I / me |
| du | dich | dir | you *(one person, informal)* |
| er | ihn | ihm | he / him |
| sie | sie | ihr | she / her |
| es | es | ihm | it |
| wir | uns | uns | we / us |
| ihr | euch | euch | you *(a group, informal)* |
| sie | sie | ihnen | they / them |
| Sie | Sie | Ihnen | you *(formal, any number)* |

> Ich sehe **dich**. — I see you.
> Er gibt **mir** das Buch. — He gives me the book.

## Possessivpronomen (Possessives) — "my, your…", then ein-word endings

| German | English |
|---|---|
| mein | my |
| dein | your *(informal sg)* |
| sein | his / its |
| ihr | her / their |
| unser | our |
| euer | your *(informal pl)* |
| Ihr | your *(formal)* |

> **mein** Hund — my dog · **meine** Katze — my cat *(endings follow ein/kein, see [[Unbestimmte]])*
