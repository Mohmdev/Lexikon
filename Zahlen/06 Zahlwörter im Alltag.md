# Zahlwörter im Alltag — number-words in everyday use

Beyond counting and ranking, German spins numbers into a small family of **derived words** — "twice",
"threefold", "in pairs", "the eighties". Each is a tidy suffix on a number you already know. Collect
them and your speech instantly sounds more native.

---

## How many times → -mal

Add **-mal** to a cardinal for "× times". One word, no spaces:

> **einmal** (once) · **zweimal** (twice) · **dreimal** (3×) · **hundertmal** (a hundred times)
> Ich war **dreimal** in Berlin. — I've been to Berlin three times.

Related: **das erste Mal** (the first time — with an **ordinal**, here *Mal* is a separate noun),
*zum **zweiten Mal*** (for the second time), **manchmal** (sometimes), **diesmal** (this time).

---

## How many fold → -fach

**-fach** = "-fold / times as much":

> **einfach** (simple / single) · **zweifach** / **doppelt** (twofold, double) · **dreifach** (triple) ·
> **mehrfach** (multiple times) · **vielfach** (manifold)

> die **doppelte** Menge — double the amount.

---

## In a group of → zu + collective

For "(all) together in a group of N", use **zu** + a special form:

> **zu zweit** (the two of us / in twos) · **zu dritt** (in threes) · **zu viert** (in fours)
> Wir waren **zu viert**. — There were four of us.

*(Note: **zu zweit** ≠ **zweitens**. zweitens = "secondly" when listing points — *erstens, zweitens,
drittens…*.)*

---

## Of N kinds → -erlei

**-erlei** = "of N different sorts" (invariable):

> **zweierlei** Käse (two kinds of cheese) · **allerlei** (all sorts of) · **keinerlei** (no … whatsoever)

---

## Decades and eras → -er

For "the eighties" etc., add **-er** to the ten (invariable):

> die **Achtzigerjahre** / die **80er-Jahre** / kurz: die **80er** — the eighties
> Musik aus den **Neunzigern** — music from the nineties
> Also for ages: *ein Mann **in den Dreißigern*** — a man in his thirties.

---

## Rough quantities (not exact)

Handy "fuzzy number" words for when you don't count precisely:

| German | meaning |
|---|---|
| **ein paar** | a few *(small p — ≠ **ein Paar** = a pair/couple, capital P)* |
| **etwa / ungefähr / circa (ca.)** | approximately |
| **rund / gut / knapp** | roughly / a good / just under (*knapp 100* = just under 100) |
| **zig / dutzende / hunderte** | dozens / hundreds (vague "loads") |
| **ein Dutzend** | a dozen (12) |

> ⚠️ **ein paar** (a few) vs **ein Paar** (a pair) — only the capital letter tells them apart:
> *ein paar Schuhe* = a few shoes; *ein Paar Schuhe* = one pair of shoes.

---

## The shape in one line

Number + suffix gives you the everyday family: **-mal** (times), **-fach** (-fold), **zu + -t** (in a
group), **-erlei** (kinds), **-er** (decades) — plus the fuzzy words (*ein paar, etwa, knapp*). All
built on the cardinals from [[01 Kardinalzahlen]].
