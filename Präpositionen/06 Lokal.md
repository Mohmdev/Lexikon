# Lokale Präpositionen — *to* a place and *from* a place

[[03 Wechsel]] covers *where something sits* (in/an/auf + case). This page covers **"to"** and
**"from"**: English has one word for each; German selects among several based on *what kind of place*
it is.

---

## Going TO a place

English uses "to"; German selects by place type:

| use **this** | for… | example |
|---|---|---|
| **nach** | cities, countries (no article), directions, *home* | **nach** Berlin, **nach** Deutschland, **nach** links, **nach** Hause |
| **zu** + Dat | people, and general destinations you head *to* | **zu** mir, **zum** Arzt, **zur** Schule, **zur** Arbeit |
| **in** + Akk | going *into* an enclosed space; articled countries | **in die** Stadt, **ins** Kino, **in die** Schweiz |
| **auf** + Akk | public places & events (open/official) | **auf die** Party, **auf den** Markt, **auf die** Bank |
| **an** + Akk | edges, water | **ans** Meer, **an den** See, **ans** Fenster |

Most frequent: **nach** for cities/countries/home, **zu** for people and everyday errands.

> Ich fahre **nach** Berlin. — I'm going to Berlin. *(city → nach)*
> Ich gehe **zum** Arzt. — I'm going to the doctor. *(destination/person → zu)*
> Ich gehe **ins** Kino. — I'm going to the cinema. *(into an enclosed space → in + Akk)*

⚠️ **nach vs zu**: *nach* is for place-names with no article (nach Berlin, nach Italien). *zu* is for
people and named destinations (zum Bahnhof, zu Oma). When a country takes an article, switch to **in**:
*in **die** Türkei*, not ~~nach der Türkei~~.

---

## Coming FROM a place — aus vs. von

| use | for… | example |
|---|---|---|
| **aus** + Dat | *out of* an enclosed space; your **origin** (home town/country) | **aus dem** Haus, Ich komme **aus** Spanien |
| **von** + Dat | from a **point, person, or surface** you were *at* | **von der** Arbeit, **von** meinem Freund, **vom** Arzt |

> Ich komme **aus** Deutschland. — I'm from Germany. *(origin → aus)*
> Ich komme gerade **von der** Arbeit. — I'm just coming from work. *(a point I was at → von)*

Rule of thumb: **aus** = out of the inside / where you're *from*; **von** = away from a point or
person.

---

## Being AT a place (static)

For *resting at* a location, the Wechsel prepositions switch to **Dativ** (*in der Stadt, am See, auf
dem Markt* — see [[03 Wechsel]]). Two extras you need:

- **bei** + Dat = **at (someone's place) / at (a workplace)**: *bei mir* (at my place), *beim Arzt* (at
  the doctor's), *bei der Arbeit* (at work). *(see [[02 Dativ]])*
- contrast **zu** (going) vs **bei** (being): *Ich gehe **zu** Anna* (to Anna's) → *Ich bin **bei** Anna*
  (at Anna's).

---

## The "Haus" set

Three fixed phrases, no article, each with a different preposition:

| German | English | which |
|---|---|---|
| **nach Hause** | (going) home | direction → nach |
| **zu Hause** | at home | location → zu |
| **von zu Hause** | from home | origin → von |

> Ich gehe **nach Hause**. — I'm going home. · Ich bin **zu Hause**. — I'm at home.

---

## Direction without a place: hin / her

When you just mean "(to) there / (to) here" with no named place, that's the **hin / her** system —
*wohin / woher*, *rein / raus / rauf / runter* — covered in [[06 hin und her]].

---

Summary: **nach** = cities/countries/home, **zu** = people/errands (going); **aus** = origin,
**von** = a point you left (coming from).
