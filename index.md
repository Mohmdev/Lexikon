# Deutsch — start here

German is a small **engine** running over per-word **data**. The engine is a handful of rules you learn once; the data is the vocabulary you absorb over time. Most of learning German is just sorting what you meet into *compute it* (a rule) vs. *memorize it* (a raw fact) — and only grinding the second kind.

This is the map, foundational-first.

## The engine — shared grammar

Learn this first. Everything below rides on it.

- [[Grammatik/00-root|Grammatik]] — **cases** (the job-tag every word carries: doer / target / receiver / owner), word order (where the verb sits), and the free wins English already hands you.

The closed sets that plug straight into cases:

- [[Artikel]] — *der / die / das*, the grid that pronouns and adjective endings copy.
- [[Präpositionen]] — each one forces a case (*mit + Dativ*, *für + Akkusativ*).
- [[Pronomen]] — *ich / mich / mir …*, learned by case.

## The words — running on the engine

**Naming & describing**

- [[Nomen]] — nouns. Always learn the triple: article + singular + plural (*das Haus, die Häuser*).
- [[Adjektive]] — describing words and their endings.
- [[Adverbien]] — how / when / where / why.

**Connecting & flavoring**

- [[Konjunktionen]] — joining words, sorted by what they do to the verb.
- [[Partikeln]] — tone words (*doch, mal, ja*) with no clean English equivalent.
- [[Interjektionen]] — *ja / nein / doch* and exclamations.

**Counting, asking, doing**

- [[Zahlen]] — numbers.
- [[Fragewörter|Fragewörter]] — question words (the W-words).
- [[Verben]] — verbs.
