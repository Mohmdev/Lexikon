# Ein Wort, viele Rollen — the same word, different jobs

The reason particles feel slippery: the most common ones are **homonyms across word classes**. *doch* is
four different things; *denn, schon, nur, mal, ja* each wear several hats. Good news — **position and
stress** tell them apart every time. This page is the untangler.

> Two clues do all the work: **where** the word sits in the clause, and **whether it's stressed.** A
> modal particle is unstressed and lives in the middle; its homonym twins are stressed and/or sit
> elsewhere.

---

## doch — four jobs

| role | where / how | example |
|---|---|---|
| **answer word** | alone, stressed, contradicting a negative | „Kommst du nicht?" — **Doch!** ([[Antwortwörter]]) |
| **modal particle** | mid-clause, unstressed, a nudge | Komm **doch** mit! — Oh, do come. |
| **coordinating conj.** | links clauses = "but/yet" | Er ist klein, **doch** stark. ([[02 Koordinierende]]) |
| **adverb "after all"** | stressed, "and yet it did" | Er ist **doch** gekommen. — He came after all. |

---

## denn — two jobs

| role | where | example |
|---|---|---|
| **modal particle** | inside a **question**, unstressed | Was machst du **denn**? — So what are you doing? |
| **coordinating conj.** | links clauses = "because" | Ich bleibe, **denn** ich bin müde. ([[02 Koordinierende]]) |

Litmus: in a **question** → particle; **joining two clauses with a reason** → conjunction.

---

## schon — two jobs

| role | how | example |
|---|---|---|
| **adverb "already"** | stressed, real time meaning | Ich bin **schon** fertig. — I'm already done. |
| **modal particle** | unstressed, reassurance | Das wird **schon**. — It'll be fine. |

---

## nur — two jobs

| role | how | example |
|---|---|---|
| **focus particle "only"** | scopes the next element | Ich habe **nur** zehn Euro. ([[04 Fokuspartikeln]]) |
| **modal particle** | in a wish/warning, urgency | Wenn er **nur** käme! — If only he'd come! |

---

## mal — two jobs

| role | how | example |
|---|---|---|
| **modal particle** | softens a request | Hilf mir **mal**. — Just help me a sec. |
| **number word "times"** | counting / math | drei**mal** (3×); zwei **mal** drei ([[06 Zahlwörter im Alltag]]) |

---

## ja — two jobs

| role | how | example |
|---|---|---|
| **answer word "yes"** | alone, stressed | „Kommst du?" — **Ja.** |
| **modal particle** | mid-clause, unstressed | Das ist **ja** toll! — Well, that's great! |

---

## The one test that always works

When you meet one of these words, ask:

1. **Is it stressed?** Stressed → it's the "real" word (answer / adverb / focus). Unstressed → likely a
   modal particle.
2. **Where does it sit?** Front or joining two clauses → conjunction/adverb. Tucked in the **middle**,
   adding tone → modal particle.

Stress + position resolve every case.

---

## The shape in one line

The frequent particles are **multi-class homonyms**; **stress** (unstressed = modal particle) and
**position** (middle = modal particle) disambiguate them every time. That's the last piece of the
particle puzzle. → back to [[Partikeln]]
