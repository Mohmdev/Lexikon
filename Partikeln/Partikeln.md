# Partikeln — the little words that carry tone

**Partikel** is German's catch-all class for **uninflectable little words** — no endings, no case, no
gender. Most subtypes you've already met elsewhere (degree words → [[Gradadverbien]], negation →
[[04 Negation]], infinitive *zu* → [[06 Infinitive Clauses]], yes/no answers → [[Interjektionen]]). What
lives **only here** is the pair that makes German *sound* German:

> **Modalpartikeln** color the tone of the whole sentence (*ja, doch, mal, denn, halt, wohl*).
> **Fokuspartikeln** spotlight one element of it (*auch, sogar, nur, erst*).

These are optional for **correctness** but essential for sounding **natural** — which is exactly why
textbooks skip them and learners sound stiff. This folder takes them seriously.

## The one-line takeaway

Particles are **uninflectable tone-words**. Learn the **Modalpartikeln** by function (*ja, doch, mal,
denn, halt, wohl* first) and the **Fokuspartikeln** by position — and use **stress + position** to tell
each from its everyday twin. Get these and you cross the line from *correct* to *natural*.
