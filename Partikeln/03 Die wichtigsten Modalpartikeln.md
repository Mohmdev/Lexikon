# Die wichtigsten Modalpartikeln — the catalog

The high-frequency flavor words, each with its **attitude**, its **home sentence type**, and an example.
Read the rules first ([[02 Modalpartikeln]]); this is the reference you come back to. Remember: the
middle column is the **feel**, not a translation.

---

## The top tier — learn these first

### ja — "as you know" / mild surprise *(statements)*
Signals the info is **obvious or shared**, or marks surprise:

> Das ist **ja** toll! — Well, that's great! *(surprise)*
> Du weißt **ja**, wie er ist. — You know, of course, how he is. *(shared knowledge)*

### doch — "but surely" / gentle insistence *(statements & commands)*
Pushes back against an expectation, or warms up a command:

> Komm **doch** mit! — Oh come on, come along! *(friendly nudge)*
> Das hab ich dir **doch** gesagt! — But I *did* tell you! *(contradicting)*

### mal — "just" / softener *(requests & commands)*
Makes an order casual and low-pressure — the single most useful particle for not sounding bossy:

> Kannst du mir **mal** helfen? — Could you just help me a sec?
> Warte **mal**! — Hang on a moment!

### denn — warmth in a question *(questions only)*
Turns a bald question into a friendly/curious one. Without it, questions can sound like interrogation:

> Was machst du **denn**? — So what are you up to? *(curious, warm)*
> Wo ist er **denn**? — Where *is* he, then?

### halt / eben — "that's just how it is" *(statements)*
Resignation, accepting an unchangeable fact. *halt* is more southern/casual, *eben* a touch sharper:

> Es ist **halt** so. — It just is what it is.
> Dann müssen wir **eben** warten. — Then we'll just have to wait.

## The second tier — add these next

### schon — reassurance / "it'll be fine" *(statements)*
> Das wird **schon**. — It'll work out. · Er kommt **schon** noch. — He'll come, don't worry.

### wohl — "probably / I suppose" *(statements)*
Marks a guess (overlaps the *werden + wohl* supposition in [[07 Futur]]):

> Er ist **wohl** krank. — He's probably ill.

### eigentlich — "actually / by the way" *(questions & statements)*
Softly introduces a new angle or a casual aside:

> Wie spät ist es **eigentlich**? — What time is it, actually?

### ruhig — "feel free to / go ahead" *(commands)*
Reassures that an action is fine — permission with no pressure:

> Du kannst **ruhig** fragen. — Feel free to ask. *(it's no problem)*

### bloß / nur — urgency in a warning or wish *(commands & wishes)*
Intensifies a warning ("just don't!") or a wish:

> Mach das **bloß** nicht! — Don't you dare do that!
> Wenn er **nur** käme! — If only he'd come! *(wish — with [[14 Konjunktiv II]])*

### etwa — "you don't mean…?" *(yes/no questions)*
Signals the speaker **fears/expects a yes** and hopes for no:

> Hast du das **etwa** vergessen? — Don't tell me you forgot?

## The shape in one line

A dozen high-value words, each = **one attitude in one sentence type**. Master *ja, doch, mal, denn,
halt, wohl* first — they cover most everyday tone. Then watch out for their **other jobs** →
[[05 Ein Wort, viele Rollen]].
