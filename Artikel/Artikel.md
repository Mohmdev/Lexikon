# Artikel (articles)

An article is the word before a noun: *the* dog, *a* dog. English has two (*the*, *a*) and they never
change. German articles carry the noun's **gender** and **case**, so the noun itself often stays
unchanged ([[01 Kasus]]). The article therefore encodes most of the grammar of the noun phrase.

Two families:

- **Definite** — "the" → *der / die / das*. → [[01 Bestimmte]]
- **Indefinite** — "a / an" → *ein / eine*, plus its negative *kein* ("no / not a"). → [[02 Unbestimmte]]

---

## Two ending patterns

There are many determiner words — *the, a, this, every, which, no, my, your…* — but all of them copy
**one of two ending patterns**:

| family | base | also copied by | grid |
| ------ | ---- | -------------- | ---- |
| **der-words** | der / die / das | **dieser** (this), **jeder** (every), **welcher** (which), **mancher** (some), **aller** (all) | [[01 Bestimmte]] |
| **ein-words** | ein / eine | **kein** (no), and the possessives **mein, dein, sein, ihr, unser, euer** | [[02 Unbestimmte]] |

Learn **two grids** and assign each new determiner to its family. *dieser/diese/dieses* is the
*der/die/das* endings on a *dies-* stem; *mein/meine/mein* is the *ein/eine/ein* endings on a *mein-*
stem.

The two grids are almost identical: they differ in exactly **three cells**, which is the subject of
[[02 Unbestimmte]].

---

## Article choice

The two grids give the **form** of each article. Choosing **which article to use, or none**, is a
separate matter, and German differs from English in both directions (*Ich bin **Arzt*** with no
article; ***Der** Hund ist treu* with one where English has none). See [[03 Artikelgebrauch]].

## Scope of articles

- Articles make **case** visible: *der → den → dem → des* marks the noun's role even when the noun
  does not change.
- They determine the **adjective endings** that follow: a different article produces a different
  adjective ending ([[Adjektive]]).
- The der-words and ein-words are the same forms as the **possessives** ([[02 Possessive]]) and
  **demonstratives** ([[05 Demonstrative]]) in [[Pronomen]]: same grids, different context.
